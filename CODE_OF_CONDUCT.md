# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project exists to make good design knowledge open and useful to everyone. We want a
community that is welcoming, respectful, and focused on the work. We expect all
participants — in issues, pull requests, reviews, and any project space — to:

- Be respectful and assume good faith.
- Give and accept feedback about the *work*, not the person.
- Welcome newcomers and help lower the barrier to contributing.
- Disagree constructively and keep discussion evidence-based.

We do not tolerate harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language, or sustained
disruption.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces and to public spaces when an individual is
representing the project.

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behaviour privately to the maintainers via a GitHub private message
or a confidential security advisory on the repository. Reports are handled discreetly.
Maintainers may warn, remove contributions, or ban participants who violate this code.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is informed by the widely used
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/); it is adapted here in our
own words rather than reproduced.
